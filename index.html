What it does

👀

Sees what you see

Press ⌥ Space or click the circle. Lume screenshots your screen once, on demand — never in the background — and opens with Claude already telling you what it sees.

🧭

Walks you through it

Guided mode highlights the exact button to click, step by step, with a pulsing outline right on your screen.

🪄

Autopilot

Give it a goal and Lume clicks and types on your behalf — narrating every action, pausing for approval on anything risky, with a Stop button always visible.

🔑

Bring your own Claude

Use an Anthropic API key, or your Claude Pro/Max subscription via the Claude Code CLI. Your keys stay in your Mac's Keychain.

First open — one extra step

Lume isn't notarized by Apple yet (that's coming), so macOS shows a warning the first time you open it. That's expected — here's the 20-second path:
  1. Open Lume.dmg and drag Lume into Applications.
  2. Double-click Lume. macOS says it "cannot verify the app is free of malware" — click Done (not "Move to Trash").
  3. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway next to Lume, then confirm.
You only do this once. Lume will then ask for Screen Recording permission (so Claude can see your screen when you ask) — grant it, then quit and reopen Lume once, as macOS requires.

Set up in two minutes

  • Option A — API key: grab a key at console.anthropic.com, open Lume's menu-bar icon → Settings, and paste it. Pay-per-use on your Anthropic account.
  • Option B — Claude subscription: if you have Claude Pro/Max and the Claude Code CLI installed (npm install -g @anthropic-ai/claude-code, then run claude once to log in), Lume can use it automatically — no API billing. Replies are a bit slower in this mode.

Privacy, plainly

  • Your screen is captured only when you ask — a click, a hotkey, a message, or between steps of an Autopilot task you started. Never on a timer.
  • Screenshots go to Anthropic's Claude and are discarded after each request. Nothing is stored or sent anywhere else.
  • Your API key lives in the macOS Keychain, not in files.
  • A menu-bar Pause disables all capture instantly.
